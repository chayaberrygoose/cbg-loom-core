# Conduit Strain

## Description
The Loom registers a critical **Conduit Strain**, a confluence of systemic vulnerabilities and environmental decay. High-latitude terrestrial infrastructures brace for predicted **G1 Geomagnetic Storm** fluctuations, a low-grade but pervasive interference resonance impacting power grids and orbital satellite operations. Concurrently, an autonomous **Robotic Servicing Mission** has launched, a calculated countermeasure for geosynchronous satellite fragility, underscoring the precarious state of off-world data conduits. Within the veiled observation strata, **NASA's Roman Space Telescope**, with its advanced coronagraphs, readies for launch, capable of erasing stellar light to reveal exoplanetary truths, yet simultaneously exposing the limits of human perception against the vastness.

On the terrestrial plane, a **policy shift by the US EPA** threatens to accelerate environmental degradation, easing public engagement rules for industrial polluters, specifically benefiting expanding **data centers** increasingly reliant on fossil fuels. This regulatory decay is mirrored by direct ecological collapse: **France endures widespread wildfires**, necessitating the evacuation of 20,000, scorching the land with thermal-kinetic force. Agricultural integrity further buckles as new research confirms **soil organic carbon fails to mitigate crop yield losses during extreme drought**, exposing critical systemic vulnerabilities in food production. The network's foundational grid suffers palpable friction as a major **New York power line** experiences operational snags, hindering energy transmission during peak demand. This interwoven decay of physical and digital infrastructure, further underscored by the **EU's €890M fine against Google for competition breaches**, indicates a pervasive breach of regulatory order and market integrity within the very conduits of global digital exchange.
